How to Make Chicken Momo

☰

In the past, Nepal and Tibet had a vibrant trade relationship. So, Nepali merchants who were primarily from Newari community of Nepa learnt the recipe and started making a modified version of it back in their homes. Its popularity in modern day Nepal has risen because of its delicious taste. There is almost no Nepali restaurant across the globe where you would not find Momo in their menus! So, I would like to present the recipe of this famous Nepali dish to you. Although it requires many ingredients and a lot of effort, you will definitely find it worth at the end.

Preparing the Dough

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour, and some extra for dusting
  • 1 tablespoon of oil
  • A pinch of salt
  • Water as needed to keand and make the dough strechy

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, oil and salt.
  2. Add water and knead the dough well.
  3. Cover and leave the dough for about 30 minutes.

Alternatively, if you want to reduce some efforts, you could purchase one of these dumpling wrappers/skin in a Chinese grocery store. My wife prefers this alternative as it saves some time and efforts.

Prepping for the Filling Mix

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs of ground chicken
  • 6 ozs of finely chopped red onion
  • 3 ozs of finely chopped green onion
  • 1 ozs of finely chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 oz of minced garlic
  • A slice of minced ginger
  • 1/2 tablespoon of turmeric
  • 1 tablespoon of Momo Masala
  • 2-5 fresh chillis (green or red) minced
  • 1 tablespoon of salt

Directions

  1. In another large bowl, combine all filing ingredients.
  2. Mix all the ingredients well.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for about an hour.

Making the Momo Sauce

Ingredients

  • 2 fairly big-sized red tomatos
  • 1 ozs of sesame seed
  • 1/2 oz of finely chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 tablespoon of Sichuan pepper
  • 2-3 fresh green chillis (green or red)

Directions

  1. Toast the white sesame seeds in a medium frying pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until nice and golden.
  2. Cut each tomato into two pieces and put them in the grill with the cut side up.
  3. After the skin of the tomato pieces are slightly burnt, remove from the grill.
  4. Combine all the ingredients into grinder, and grind.

Putting Everything Together..

  1. Give the dough a final knead (Follow steps 1-9 only if you chose to make the dough yourself).
  2. Prepare about 1-inch dough balls.
  3. Take a ball, roll between your palms to spherical shape.
  4. Dust working board with some flour.
  5. Gently flatten the ball with your palm to about 2-in circle.
  6. Roll the flatten ball with rolling pin into a wrapper until it attains 3-in diameter.
  7. Continue step 2-7 for the remaining dough.
  8. Place wrappers in a bowl making sure that they do not stick together.
  9. Cover them so that they do not dry.
  10. Now, take each wrapper in one hand, and start filling with a tablespoon of the filling mix.
  11. With another hand, bring all edges together to center making the pleats.
  12. Pinch and twist the pleats to ensure that the stuffed dumpling is closed tightly.
  13. Heat up a steamer and oil the steamer rack well before placing the dumplings.
  14. When the water starts to boil, put the steamer rack on the steamer and close the lid.
  15. Leave the dumlings for 12 minutes.
  16. Once done, take the dumplings off the steamer and serve them immediately.

Give yourself a pat on your shoulder for all the hard work. Hope you enjoy the juicy and delicious Momos!
